## Design Context

### Users
This project serves two closely related audiences inside Masterminds HQ:

1. Workshop participants
They are small business owners, consultants, coaches, and service operators who are new to building with AI. Most are absolute beginners when it comes to coding. They come to the workshop site for session guides, prep pages, homework, and follow-along implementation support. Their job to be done is to build something real without getting intimidated or lost.

2. Giveaway visitors
They arrive through free resources such as the Web Design Arsenal, safety checklist, benchmark, and lead magnet pages. They may not be members yet. Their job to be done is to get immediate tactical value, feel that this is practical and credible, and see a clear path into the Masterminds HQ ecosystem.

The interface should make both groups feel:
- confident
- guided
- capable
- energized
- clear on the next step

### Brand Personality
Three-word personality:
- practical
- premium
- human

Voice and emotional goals:
- Clear and direct, never vague or hypey
- Warm and encouraging without sounding childish
- High taste, but grounded in usefulness and execution
- Premium enough to feel valuable, never ornamental for its own sake

### Aesthetic Direction
This project should keep the current Masterminds workshop aesthetic:
- dark background
- cream text
- purple and soft pink accents
- subtle motion
- modern, polished, AI-native feel

Reference direction:
- premium workshop portal
- high-signal tactical giveaway landing pages
- guided product experience rather than editorial luxury branding

Anti-reference:
- Do not make this feel like a luxury brand site, hospitality site, fashion site, resort site, or high-end lifestyle brochure.
- Avoid visual cues that read as "new luxury website" such as oversized editorial whitespace, ultra-minimal beige-on-beige palettes, aspirational resort photography, or prestige-first art direction.
- Avoid abstract, mysterious, or overly conceptual design moves that reduce clarity for beginners.

Theme split inside the same project:
- Workshop pages should feel guided, structured, and teacher-led.
- Giveaway pages can be more attention-grabbing and conversion-oriented, but must still feel connected to the workshop ecosystem.

### Design Principles
1. Teach without intimidation.
Every page should reduce friction and make the next action obvious, especially for non-technical users.

2. Premium means polished, not luxurious.
Use strong hierarchy, smooth motion, and crisp visual systems. Do not drift into luxury aesthetics.

3. Clarity beats cleverness.
Content-heavy pages should stay scannable, instructional, and outcome-focused.

4. Giveaways should earn trust fast.
Lead with immediate value, clear proof of usefulness, and a strong bridge back to Masterminds HQ.

5. Motion should support understanding.
Animations, reveals, counters, and effects should reinforce flow and energy, not distract from the teaching.

6. Keep the two modes distinct but related.
Workshop pages should feel like a guided program. Giveaway pages should feel like high-value entry points into that program.
